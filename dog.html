How to know your dog is poisoned

14-Feb-2024

Have you ever come across a poisoned dog? If so, what does it look like? Each year a quite number of dogs are lost to poisoning. It may be due to what the dog ate or somebody poisoned your dog. Dogs get into anything they come across, which is a potential risk to their health. It's very important to know the signs of dog poisoning.

Signs of a poisoned dog

Symptoms of dog poisoning vary depending on what substance your dog has taken. You need to be extra careful about what your dog takes. Below are some of the signs:
Vomiting
Look for signs of continuous vomiting, because dogs vomit but if you see a persistent trend of vomiting your dog could be poisoned. Most dogs when vomiting tend to go far away so that they can vomit, so watch out, and if the vomiting continues your dog could lose appetite
Diarrhoea
When your dog diarrhoeas or you find blood in the stool, either bright red blood or dark black tarry stools your dog could be poisoned. Diarrhoea could lead to stress in your dog, and also changing diet for your dog could lead to diarrhoea so make food – transition so that your dog can adapt to the food slowly
Dehydration
Dehydration occurs when more fluid is lost. It can cause fatigue in your dog as it will appear tired and not active. The purpose of water for pets is to carry important nutrients into and out of the cells of the body, which aids in digestion and the absorption of nutrients.
Behavioural changes
You will instantly realize the changes in your dog for instance the dog will lie most of the times, foaming at the mouth and most probably your dog will be dull. When you try giving food you will realize that there is a loss of appetite, the dog only snuffs the food. Even the walking style will change as most of the dogs will walk the head facing down, implying it's not feeling well. I would recommend you give your dog some water in order to recover the water lost either through vomiting or diarrhoea You may not be able to see other signs like liver failure and unusual heartbeat
Common poisonous substances and foods to Lookout For:
Look out for these substances that can poison your dog and pose a potential danger to your dog's health. Chemicals and insecticides. The metabolism process in dogs is different from that of humans:
Medication for humans like blood pressure pills or antidepressants
Human foods that are extremely dangerous like garlic, onions, or chocolate
Medication for humans like ibuprofen, acetaminophen
Bleach from cleaners
Plants like tulips or holly

How to handle poisoning
It's important to protect your pet in case of an emergency like poisoning;
visit the vet immediately
Collect the samples or you can take a clear photo of what you suspect could be the poison
The vet work is to prevent any further absorption of the substance into your pet’s body like the injection of fluid into the lower bowel to flush the digestive system
