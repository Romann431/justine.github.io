Top 5 Coffee-Producing Countries in the World

23-Jan-2024

Have you ever wondered which of these countries produce the best coffee?

Top coffee producing countries.

Brazil
Brazil is by far the worlds biggest producer of coffee. It produces 2,875,974 tones as per 2017.That means a third of the total production worldwide. Coffee generates 8 million jobs in the country, making it a driving force in the Country's economy.


Vietnam
Vietnam comes in second place after Brazil, with Robusta coffee. Accounting to 97 percent of Vietnams total output Robusta coffee today, Coffee is an integral part of Vietnamese culture, and people of all ages and social classes. Enjoy drinking it. Starbuck opened its first store in Vietnam setting a trend for global retailers to follow suit, names like Boncafe, the European producer of gourment café.
Columbia
Columbia comes a distant third in coffee production. It produces mostly Arabica bean and the beans are mostly exported to the united states, France , Japan and Italy. Its no surprise in 2011, UNESCO declared the “ Coffee Cultural Landscape” of Columbia, a world Heritage Site
Indonesia
It is estimated that Indonesia produced 660,000 metric tones of coffee in 2017 . Sumatra Mandheling Coffee is produced outside the city of Padang, in the west coast coffee district. The Arabica coffee is mostly grown in the Indonesian island of Sulawesi which lies to the east of Borno Island
Ethiopia
Ethiopia is found in the sub-Saharan Africa. Ethiopia produces the best coffee in Africa because of its high elevations in the southern mountainous regions create excellent growing conditions . The most commonly grown type of coffee in Ethiopia is coffee Arabica and people depend on the coffee for their livelihood.
